What is AIFMD?

The Alternative Investment Fund Managers Directive (AIFMD) is the EU's regulatory framework for managers of alternative investment funds (AIFs). It covers hedge funds, private equity funds, real estate funds, and other non-UCITS investment vehicles.

AIFMD requires fund managers to register with national regulators, report detailed fund information via Annex IV filings, maintain robust risk management frameworks, and provide transparent investor disclosures. The recently adopted AIFMD II expands these requirements further.

Key Facts

Effective

2013 (AIFMD II: 2026)

Applies to

EU-based AIFMs + non-EU marketing in EU

Key Filing

Annex IV (quarterly/semi-annual)

Regulator

National Competent Authorities + ESMA

Why AIFMD Compliance is Hard

Complex Annex IV

300+ data fields across multiple fund profiles, reported quarterly or semi-annually.

Leverage Calculation

Multiple leverage methodologies (gross/commitment) with different inclusion rules.

Risk Management

Stress testing, liquidity management, and risk reporting requirements across market, credit, and counterparty risks.

AIFMD II Changes

New requirements for loan origination, liquidity tools, delegation rules, and enhanced reporting.
