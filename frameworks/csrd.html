What is CSRD?

The Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD) is the EU's landmark regulation requiring companies to disclose detailed information about their environmental, social, and governance (ESG) impacts. It replaces and significantly expands the Non-Financial Reporting Directive (NFRD).

CSRD mandates reporting according to the European Sustainability Reporting Standards (ESRS), covering topics from climate change and biodiversity to workforce conditions and business conduct. Reports must be digitally tagged in XBRL format and subject to limited assurance by auditors.

Key Facts

Effective

FY 2024 (large companies)

Applies to

~50,000 EU companies

Standards

ESRS (12 standards)

Penalties

Up to €10M or 5% revenue

Why CSRD Compliance is Hard

1,000+ Data Points

ESRS requires collection of over 1,000 individual data points across ESG topics.

Double Materiality

Companies must assess both financial and impact materiality — a complex, subjective process.

Scope 3 Emissions

Tracking supply chain emissions requires data from hundreds of suppliers — most don't have it ready.

Tight Deadlines

Different company sizes have different deadlines. Preparation typically takes 6-12 months.
