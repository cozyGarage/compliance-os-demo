NIS2

Network and Information Security Directive 2

What is NIS2?

The Network and Information Security Directive 2 (NIS2) is the EU's updated cybersecurity legislation, replacing the original NIS Directive. It significantly expands the scope of sectors and entities subject to cybersecurity requirements.

NIS2 classifies entities as "essential" or "important" across 18 sectors, mandating risk management measures, incident reporting (24h early warning, 72h full notification), supply chain security, and management accountability. Non-compliance can result in fines up to €10M or 2% of global turnover.

Key Facts

Transposition Deadline

October 17, 2024

Scope

18 sectors, ~160,000 entities

Incident Reporting

24h early warning, 72h notification

Penalties

Up to €10M or 2% turnover

Why NIS2 Compliance is Hard

24h Incident Reporting

Significant incidents must be reported within 24 hours — requiring rapid detection and response capabilities.

Supply Chain Security

Must assess and manage cybersecurity risks from suppliers and service providers across the entire supply chain.

Management Liability

C-suite and board members can be held personally liable for non-compliance — cybersecurity governance training is mandatory.

Technical Measures

Requires encryption, MFA, vulnerability management, access control, and business continuity plans across all systems.
